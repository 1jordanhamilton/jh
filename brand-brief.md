## 5. Typography Direction

| Role | Font | Weight | Rationale |
|------|------|--------|-----------|
| **Headings** | **Inter** | 700-800 | The standard for precision-engineered tech interfaces. Tight letter-spacing at large sizes creates the Linear/Vercel feel. Geometric but not cold. Available on Google Fonts, zero load-time risk. |
| **Body** | **Inter** | 400 | Single-family stack keeps the design disciplined. Inter's x-height and spacing are optimized for screen readability at 15-16px body size. |
| **Metrics/Numbers** | **Inter Tight** or **JetBrains Mono** | 700 | Monospace or tight-set numbers for key stats ($150M, 150%, $25M ARR) gives them visual weight and a data-dashboard feel that reinforces technical credibility. |

**Hierarchy:**
- H1: 40-48px, -0.8px letter-spacing, weight 800
- H2: 24-28px, -0.4px letter-spacing, weight 700
- Body: 15-16px, normal letter-spacing, weight 400, line-height 1.6
- Metric callouts: 20-28px, monospace or tight, weight 700

## 9. Differentiator Framework

### "Full-Stack Marketing Leadership"

The concept: the best marketing executives have operated at every layer — SDR, digital, demand gen, operations, brand, exec strategy. This full-stack experience is what separates leaders who can actually build from those who can only direct.

**Why it works as a framework:**
- It's nameable and memorable (like "T2D3" or "Bullseye Marketing")
- It directly maps to your career arc (SDR → Coordinator → Manager → Director → VP)
- It answers the AI question naturally: full-stack operators know where AI amplifies real work vs. where it's theater
- It differentiates you from career-track CMOs who went MBA → consultant → director → VP without touching the tools

**How to use it:**
- Reference in your hero or POV section: "I practice what I call full-stack marketing leadership..."
- Use the stack metaphor visually in the timeline — layers building on each other
- Tie it to your AI POV: "Full-stack operators see where AI actually moves the needle"

## 10. Anti-Guidelines

| Avoid | Why |
|-------|-----|
| **Stock photography** | Instantly reads as "consultant website." Use actual headshot, data visualizations, or no imagery at all. |
| **Corporate language** | No "synergy," "holistic," "leverage," "thought leader," "passionate about." These words signal that you have nothing specific to say. |
| **Wall-of-text bios** | Let metrics and proof points carry the weight. If it takes more than 3 sentences to explain what you do, the positioning isn't sharp enough. |
| **Hustle-culture signals** | No follower counts, no "featured in" logo bars (unless Fortune 50), no countdown timers, no "book a call" popups. |
| **Overdesigned flourishes** | No parallax scrolling, no animated backgrounds, no gradient meshes. The Linear/Vercel aesthetic wins through restraint, not decoration. |
| **AI as identity** | AI is in your toolkit, not your title. "AI Marketing Expert" reads as a gimmick in 2026. "Marketing executive with AI fluency" reads as durable. Position AI as how you work, not what you are. |
| **Generic blue** | The B2B SaaS space is drowning in #3B82F6 corporate blue. The indigo accent (#4F46E5) shifts just enough to signal difference while staying in the trust range. |
